# M+ Museum Hong Kong Website UI/UX Summary  
**Website:** https://www.mplus.org.hk/tc  
**Purpose:** Reference for building a similar modern museum / cultural institution website

## Overall Impression
Clean, minimalist, elegant and visually immersive contemporary museum website.  
Prioritizes large-scale high-quality art photography, generous whitespace, smooth scrolling, and fast content discovery.  
Feels like a digital art gallery — artistic yet highly functional.  
Fully responsive (desktop-first, excellent mobile experience).  
Bilingual (Traditional Chinese / English) with seamless language switching.

## 1. Header (Sticky Top Bar)
- Simple “M+” wordmark logo on the far left
- Main navigation (centered or right-aligned):
  - Visit / Get Tickets
  - Exhibitions
  - Events
  - Collection
  - Magazine
  - Cinema
  - Membership
- Right side utilities:
  - Language toggle (EN / TC)
  - Search icon
  - Prominent **“Get Tickets”** button (high-contrast CTA)
- Mobile: Hamburger menu that expands into full-screen or side drawer
- Thin top banner for cookie consent and opening hours note

## 2. Hero Section (Full-bleed, Tall)
- Massive cinematic background image or subtle slider (museum architecture or current exhibition artwork)
- Large centered overlay text (white, bold sans-serif)
  - Main headline welcoming visitors
  - Sub-headline with practical information (e.g., opening hours, “closed on Mondays”)
- Big, bold **“Get Tickets”** CTA buttons
- Subtle scroll prompt at the bottom

## 3. Main Content Sections (Vertical Scroll)
All sections are full-width with consistent card-based layout:

### Exhibitions
- Horizontal carousel or 3–4 column grid
- Each card: large hero image + title + dates + “Free Entry” badge (when applicable)
- “All Exhibitions” link at the end

### Events
- Similar card grid showing date, time, and title
- “All Events” link

### M+ Magazine
- Grid or horizontal scroll of article cards
- Thumbnail + title + author + read time + category tags
- “Read More” link

### Collection Online
- Teaser block: “Discover 10,000+ objects”
- Prominent “Explore More” button

### M+ Cinema
- Promo section highlighting programmes and the iconic M+ Facade LED screen
- “View Programmes” link

### Membership
- Benefits shown in grid/icons (Free Access, Priority Entry, Exclusive Events, Lounge, Private Viewings, Discounts, etc.)
- Strong “Join Today” / “立即加入” CTA

### Newsletter Signup
- Simple subscription form with benefit bullets
- “Subscribe” button

**Card Design (consistent across site):**
- Generous image ratio
- Rounded or sharp modern corners
- Hover effects: subtle scale (105%), shadow lift, quick info fade-in

## 4. Interactive Elements & Micro-animations
- Smooth carousels with arrows and dots (auto-play on desktop)
- Hover states on all cards
- Section fade-in animations on scroll
- Lazy-loaded high-resolution images
- Excellent performance and accessibility (clear focus states, alt texts)

## 5. Footer
- Multi-column layout:
  - About M+
  - Visit Information
  - Programmes (Exhibitions, Cinema, Events)
  - Support Us / Membership
  - Magazine & Collection
- Social media icons
- Links to West Kowloon Cultural District, contact, privacy, copyright
- Repeated newsletter signup option

## 6. Design System
**Color Palette:**
- Base: White / off-white background with dark text
- Accents: Vibrant colors pulled from exhibition artwork
- CTAs: Bold black or deep accent colors

**Typography:**
- Modern clean sans-serif (optimized for both English and Chinese)
- Hero headlines: very large, bold, tight tracking
- Generous line height and whitespace

## 7. UX Strengths
- Instant focus on “Get Tickets” and current exhibitions
- Visual-first discovery flow (image → details)
- Perfect bilingual experience
- Strong membership promotion with clear value
- Mobile experience feels native
- Elegant yet never cluttered — artistic and functional

---

**Notes for Replication:**
This site is built to feel like a digital extension of the physical museum: elegant, image-heavy, easy to navigate, and conversion-oriented (tickets & membership).  
Use the structure, card styles, hover effects, and hierarchy above as direct reference when building your version.
